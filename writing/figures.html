Advice

Here’s a bunch of generic figure feedback

Use sans serif fonts for figures to distinguish figure text from the serif fonts normally used in papers.

For overview and schematic figures, unless each color has a distinct meaning, picking a color scheme of 3-4 colors and sticking with it is better than using a different color for everything. I like to flip through coolors.io/generate and press space until I see a nice color scheme.

Same thing with shapes. Try not to have rounded rects, hexagons, dashed lines, solid lines, lines of different widths, etc unless they convey semantic meaning. Consistency is better.

For preserving space in a paper, I like making charts more like a 4:1 ratio so that it can be full width but not take up too much height. For presentations, you might be used to making 16:9 figures. For papers, I go with either wider figures (full width), or almost square figures (single column, often combined with other square figures to form a row of three square figures).
