Resolution in Neural Networks

Pix2Struct

Simply encode an image with a maximum sequence length and use learned positional embeddings. This handles differently sized resolutions by allowing for shorter sequences. However, I think in practice everything still gets resized down.

They show that this is better than adding visual padding tokens or distorting the aspect ratio for the simple task of predicting text.

Llava-1.6

Does a sort of aspect-aware splitting and then resizes and encodes the entire image and each of the four quarters. This produces 5x as many visual tokens. They flatten these tokens to a sequence and include a special “end of row” token to indicate the end of a row of features.
