How Do I Do Science

I’m working with ecologists and there’s a ton of background knowledge for me to get up to speed on. There’s also a ton of background culture for me to get up to speed on. If I want to change the way science is performed, I need to understand how it’s performed now and why (Chesterton’s Fence) before I suggest changes. That’s taking up a bunch of my mental energy because some things completely backwards and so they require deep thought before I understand it. Compared to ML/AI research, where I know the problems, the motivations, the limitations, and I can understand most papers’ motivations and inuition easily, ecology is really draining for me.

So here are some ongoing thoughts for me to continue thinking about. Basically all of this could be wrong because I’m not an ecologist.

Searching for Trends

I’m working on finding relationships between elytra size in Carabid beetles (ground beetles) and other factors, such as latitude, temperature, humidity, etc. As I understand it, the holy grail is finding something like elytra size \(\approx\) humidity with some error. We find a linear relationship with a bias term and some error/noise, but we discover a new linear relationship that holds across all beetles everywhere. This is the best. It’s also unlikely that a relationship that simple exists at all, much less has not been discovered already.

How do we find relationships? To me, this is a search problem. We have lots of variables, lots of ways to compose the variables, and methods to score the fit of each variable composition. For example, we might try the following:

  1. elytra width = humidity * beta + alpha
  2. elytra width / beetle mass = humidity * beta + alpha
  3. elytra width / beetle mass = humidity * beta + temperature * delta + alpha
  4. elytra width / beetle mass = humidity * temperature * beta + alpha
  5. …and so on.

We can compose increasingly complex relationships by adding both more variables and more interactions between variables. Some of these make sense: we probably care about elytra width with respect to body size (sort of controlling for body size). Some of them don’t: I’m not sure what humidity * temperature would indicate.

But we have a couple guiding principles:

  1. Simpler is better.
  2. Better fits are better.
  3. Plausible compsitions are better.

There are nice, easy ways to measure #1 and #2. #3 is much harder to evaluate, but I suspect could be done by an LLM with enough guidance. If you have a search space and a ranking function, this is a problem that can be easily solved.

Why would I manually go through all the possible combinations based on my own intuition, rather than do massive search and then manually evaluate the top candidates?
