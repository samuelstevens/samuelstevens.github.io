Training

Karras et al. 2023 (Analyzing and Improving the Training Dynamics of Diffusion Models) propose a new post-hoc EMA method. Typical EMA requires that you update your EMA weights every batch. Since you don’t want to store a copy of your model weights for every batch, you have to do this during training. If you decide you want different EMA hyperparameters, then you need to retrain. Karras et al.’s contribution is figuring out how to model arbitrary EMA profiles after training by taking a series of specific snapshots. EMA makes a big difference in image generation tasks. Then this paper shows that the optimal EMA hyperparameters can make a significant difference in model quality (see the figure below).

It’s not clear if EMA can lead to similar improvements for classification and non-diffusion models, but it might worth a shot.
