Improving Dichotomous Keys

Keys and the Crisis in Taxonomy: Extinction or Reinvention? presents some discussion on dichotomous key design in the context of taxonomists becoming rarer. The authors include a list of ten rules to incorporate when designing keys. Nine of the rules are UI/UX in my opinion (use images, includ contrasting options, beta-test your key, etc.). One rule is use a balanced tree whenever possible (which is a nice computer science concept). They also suggest making it easy to backtrack, and this has come up in converations with field taxonomists as well: if you end up at a classification that is clearly wrong, it’s easy to go back to the decision that you were least certain about and then take the other path. It might be interesting to incorporate some of that thinking into the tree design as well.
